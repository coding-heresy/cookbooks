* Single substitution
echo foo | sed 's/f/b/'
outputs "boo"

echo foo | sed 's/o/b/'
outputs "fbo"

* Multiple/global substitution
echo foo | sed 's/o/u/g'
outputs "fuu"

* Substitute '-' for '.' in all occurrences
echo 1.07.0000 | sed 's/\./-/g'
outputs "1-07-0000"

* Remove carriage return characters
** If ^M can be printed:
sed 's/^M$//'
** If sed supports hex escape codes:
sed 's/\x0D$//'

* Replace space with newline
sed 's/\S\+/\n/g'

Actually replaces each contiguous block of whitespace characters with
a single newline.

* Remove spaces at the end of a line
sed 's/ *$//'

* Remove all matching lines
sed '/part of a line to remove/d'

* Replace newline with space
Don't use sed, use tr.
